Admitted Students

  • How can I participate in research at the ONE lab?
    • You could browse page and participate our group meetings. You'll have the chance to explore topics the lab focuses on and meet the students involved in the lab.
    You are also encouraged to consider taking an independent study class with Dr. Wan as a way to determine if ONE lab is a good fit for you. If you perform well in the course, you may be considered for a Research Assistant (RA) position in ONE lab, though it is not a requirement for your graduate studies. Many graduate students fund their education through off-campus work, fellowships, or scholarships.
  • I need help with my paper/thesis, can you help?
    • If you are considering a collaboration or seeking a master's or doctoral thesis advisor, please feel free to email Dr. Wan (wwan@unm.edu).

Prospective Students

  • I'm applying for graduate school and intereted in your lab, can I contact you in advance?
    • Yes, feel free to email Dr. Wan with your CV, statement of insterest, unofficial transcripts from both undergraduate and higher education (if any), and 1-2 representative samples of your work (if any), which could include publications, thesis, research projects, or repositories, for his own review. But you still need to officically apply through one of the graduate programs, such as ME, ECE, and CS. You should be familiar with the specific requirements of your specific department of your degree and contact them for questions about admissions.
  • How can I be a postdoc in the ONE lab?
    • Please email to Dr. Wan with your CV and a short write-up of statement of interests with your related publications.
